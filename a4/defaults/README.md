# A4 default datasets

For anyone who'd rather not hunt for a corpus. Both are **synthetic** (generated for this course — realistic in shape, safe to send anywhere) and sized for the assignment minimums.

| File | What | Fits |
|---|---|---|
| `reviews.csv` | 800 app-store-style reviews of wellbeing/productivity apps (`review_id`, `app`, `rating`, `review_text`). Realistic mix: praise, bugs, privacy complaints, paywall anger, dubious health claims, mixed feelings — plenty of codebook-worthy ambiguity. | **Option 1** (classify by complaint type, or build your own scheme) |
| `abstracts_screening.csv` | 150 mini-abstracts (`abstract_id`, `text`); sample sizes and methods are *sometimes missing on purpose* — the A1 lesson at scale. | **Option 1** (extraction: topic, method, N, finding direction — into a screening table) |

**Images (Option 1, image flavor):** no default image set ships in the repo — good public sets are large and licensing varies by use. If you want the image option, bring a public/CC-licensed set from your own field to the Session 8 clinic (or email me before it) and we'll scope it together in five minutes.

**Own data instead?** Public data from your field is very welcome — name the source in your scope spec. Your own sensitive research data stays out, as everywhere in this course.
