## Trap map (what P0 typically gets wrong)

- **Ab. 01:** two candidate findings (23% effect; futility mediation; elimination by explanation sessions) — models often merge them or pick the mediation as "the" finding non-verbatim. Verbatim-quote discipline (V) and finding-selection judgment.
- **Ab. 02:** classic **M-trap**: no sample size anywhere ("ventures in a Northern European accelerator"). Models infer a plausible N or write "multiple ventures" — that's **I**, must be NOT REPORTED.
- **Ab. 03:** observational design with causal language ("we exploit") — models often write "experiment" (**F/I** on method). Also survey subsample is supplementary, not the main method.
- **Ab. 04:** split sample reporting — Studies 1–2 have no N, Study 3 does. Models report 28,750 as "the" sample (**M** + **I**). Also "scraped" tempts ethics commentary not asked for (**V**).
- **Ab. 05:** hedged finding — the honest verbatim quote must carry the hedge ("imprecisely estimated"). Models drop the hedge (**I**, the dangerous one). Authors explicitly warn against the risk-aversion interpretation; models state it anyway (**F**).

## Grading note

Expect P0 error totals around 8–15 across the set; a good P2 lands ≤4 with M-traps fixed and hedges preserved. Persisting errors are usually Ab. 05's hedge (model limitation — partially fixable) and Ab. 01 finding selection (fixable with an explicit selection rule). Students whose matrices show zero errors at P0 almost certainly didn't score against the key fields strictly.
